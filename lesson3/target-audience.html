Target Audience

Who

Meteorologists, journalists, college students, and others deeply interested in nature.

Age Range

18-40

Technology

Advanced technology users, familiar with complex reporting systems and databases.

Motivation

Research and cite complex historical weather data for articles, papers, and forecasting.

Personas

Dallas Raines

Dallas recently graduated and works for the local newspaper. He's 25 years old. He's grown up with technology his entire life and has even done a bit of programming in his spare time. He's used a Macbook since college but his family computer growing up was a PC. He's very curious and is always willing to go above and beyond to find interesting the next interesting news story. He would use The Weather Eye to download a csv file of historical data for his hometown for a story.

Wendy Morningdew

Wendy works as a freelance wildlife photographer for a prominent nature magazine. She spends 75% of the year travelling around the world on various assignments. Wendy is outgoing, energetic, and curious. She is familiar with technology when it comes to her photography but doesn't love really detailed reports. She mostly wants to use the website to plan out her trips and get an idea of the best time of the year to shoot in certain areas. She's interested in a tool to create to help her create some simple but informative graphs.

Scenarios

  1. What's the average rainfall between March and September in Zimbabwe over the last 10 years?
  2. When was the last major flood in Minnesota?
  3. What was the range of the last five tornadoes in Kansas
  4. Has it always rained this much in Seattle??
  5. Over the last century, what's the coldest average temperature in December for the state of Alaska?
